### De novo assembly of 160 APGp1 Y chromosomes
The majority of 160 Y-chromosome assemblies were derived from the APGp1 (Asian Pan-Genome phase1, https://github.com/Asian-Pan-Genome/APGp1) project. 

To ensure the high integrity of the primary Y assemblies, we manually examined all potential mis-assemblies, including abnormally short PAR1 (pseudoautosomal region 1) (3/160), misplacement of PAR2 fragments (12/160), and severely shortened or incorrectly haplotype-assigned heterochromatic regions (Yq12; 15/160) (Supplementary Table 3). In such cases, instead of using complementary contigs that might introduce unexpected errors, we reselected and connected the more continuous contigs generated by either Hifiasm (v0.16.1)(Cheng et al., 2021) or Verkko (v1.4.1)(Rautiainen et al., 2023) with RagTag (v2.1.0)(Alonge et al., 2022). The HG002-Y(Rhie et al., 2023) was used as the reference template. Detailed procedures for subsequent gap-filling, polishing, and manual correction were described in the APGp1 flagship manuscript (Wu et al., 2026).

### Assembly evaluation
1. **Y chromosome assembly size evaluation**

  By comparing Y chromosome size estimates derived from HiFi and PCR-free short reads with assembled sizes across samples, we evaluated the correlations to support the reliability of the Y chromosome assemblies.

2. **Base-level accuracy and continuity assessment**

  Base accuracy and structural continuity of Y chromosome assemblies were evaluated using Merqury (v1.3) (Rhie et al., 2020) QV scores and GCI (Genome Continuity Inspector, Chen et al., 2024) metrics based on HiFi and ONT read alignments, with results summarized to benchmark overall assembly quality.

3. **Mis-assembly detection and characterization**

  A combined NucFreq (v0.1) (Vollger et al., 2019) and Flagger (v0.3.2)(Liao et al., 2023) framework identified a small fraction of confident mis-assemblies and a broader set of potentially problematic regions (Supplementary Table 5), with errors predominantly enriched in highly repetitive regions such as Yq12, ampliconic regions, and centromeres.

4. **Phasing accuracy and telomere validation**

  Phasing accuracy in PAR regions was assessed using yak (v0.1-r69) (https://github.com/lh3/yak) with parental k-mers, while telomere detection revealed that the majority of Y chromosome ends contain canonical telomeric repeat motifs.
